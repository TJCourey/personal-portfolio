About Me

Hello! My name is TJ and I’m making a significant life change in order to join the world of software engineering by attending the University of Denver’s Full Stack Coding Bootcamp. This is an exciting opportunity and I’m looking forward to a rewarding career full of challenges and problem solving. In the past, I worked in both the construction and climbing gym industries. I built everything from wine cellars to cell phone towers and developed climbing programs for multiple non-profits, in addition to working for several climbing gyms. In my free time, I enjoy finding adventures in nature. Whether it is climbing, hiking, or fishing, I love the sun on my face.

Work Experience

While working in the indoor climbing industry, I organized and implemented climbing programs for multiple non-profits. As an assistant manager, I was a key member of the high school climbing league at a large climbing gym. I played an integral role in launching a Colorado Springs bouldering gym. Additionally, I consulted with several other facilities to help them develop outdoor recreation programs. At the end of 2020, I felt the need for a new challenge and decided to change careers. This need inspired me to pursue software engineering and I enrolled in the University of Denver Full Stack Coding Bootcamp. I am excited to learn new skills that will allow me solve coding problems and bring valuable skills to a tech company.

Accomplishments

  • The Phoenix: Increased attendance in the Denver area programs by 60-80 participants per week and developed national standards for best practices.
  • UpaDowna: Launched the “Climb Night” program and introduced hundreds of families and individuals to climbing, in addition to developing outdoor climbing events.
  • Team RWB: Co-created weekly climbing events, as well as organizing a yearly climbing camp in Estes Park.
  • TwoCor Projects: Developed a weekly climbing program focused on rehabilitation, as well as mentoring at-risk youth.
